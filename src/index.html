Open Academic Search (OAS) is a working group aiming to advance scientific research and discovery, promote technology that assists the scientific and academic communities, and make research available worldwide for the good of all humanity.

Our core principles

1

Collaboration drives innovation in academic search.

2

AI plays a unique role in surfacing and analyzing information in millions of research papers and academic journals.

3

Our core mission is advancing the pace of research and aiding breakthroughs in critical research areas.

Resources

Sites

  • Cornell University Library

    ArXiv

    ArXiv is an e-print service in the fields of physics, mathematics, computer science, quantitative biology, quantitative finance and statistics. Submissions to arXiv should conform to Cornell University academic standards.

  • Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence

    Citeomatic

    A deep learning model for citation prediction. New citations and references are linked and suggested from uploaded research papers (Computer science and neuroscience currently).

  • Pennsylvania State University

    CiteSeerX

    Rather than creating just another digital library, CiteSeerx attempts to provide resources such as algorithms, data, metadata, services, techniques, and software that can be used to promote other digital libraries.

  • University of Washington

    Eigenfactor

    Eigenfactor uses recent advances in network analysis to develop novel methods for evaluating the influence of scholarly periodicals, for mapping the structure of academic research, and for helping researchers navigate the scholarly literature.

  • Microsoft

    Microsoft Academic

    Microsoft Academic is a free public search engine for academic publications and literature, developed by Microsoft Research. The tool features an entirely new data structure and search engine using semantic search technologies and it currently indexes over 150 million entities.

  • Open Academic Society

    Open Academic Society

    Open Academic Society is formed by a group of institutions to create a shared, open and expanding knowledge graph of research and education-focused entities and relationships.

  • Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence

    Semantic Scholar

    Semantic Scholar leverages our AI expertise to help researchers find the most relevant information efficiently.

  • University of Washington

    Viziometrics

    Viziometrics uses techniques from computer vision and machine learning to classify more than 8 million figures from PubMed into 5 figure types and study the resulting patterns of visual information as they relate to impact.

Open Source & Data

  • University of Washington

    Babel

    An open REST API to deliver scholarly article recommendations to researchers around the world.

  • University of Washington

    FlourishOA

    FlourishOA is a data-driven web app and API enabling users to discover relevant and reputable Open Access (OA) publications in order to maximize publishing impact.

  • Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence

    Open Corpus

    Open access to Semantic Scholar’s corpus of 7.2 million research papers metadata and 6.1 million paper URLs.

  • Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence

    PDFfigures 2.0

    A software library for extracting figures from research papers online.

  • Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence

    Science Parse

    A library for extracting metadata–titles, authors, abstracts, references, and mentions of the references–from scientific papers. JVM based and flexible enough to be incorporated into any project.

  • Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence

    Semantic Scholar API

    Lightweight programmatic access to Semantic Scholar's paper and author data.
